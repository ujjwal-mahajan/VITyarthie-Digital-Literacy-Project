Name: Ujjwal Mahajan 

Registration No: 25BAI10205

Project Overview:

This repository contains my comprehensive Digital Literacy Portfolio, developed as part of the CSE0001 course at VIT Bhopal University. As a Student Digital Ambassador, I have curated a series of resources and professional profiles designed to demonstrate my ability to navigate the digital world safely, responsibly, and effectively.

The project is organized into five distinct modules, covering everything from visual communication and professional networking to coding collaboration and cybersecurity awareness. Each task serves as a practical application of digital literacy skills essential for a first-year engineering student.

Module Summaries:

Module 1: Creating a Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

Objective: To act as a Student Digital Ambassador by creating a one-page visual resource for batchmates.
Execution: Used Canva to design an infographic that translates complex digital concepts into an accessible visual format.
Core Topics: The resource covers the definition of digital literacy, a curated list of useful student tools (like Notion or Grammarly), and essential safe internet practices.Key Learning: Focused on condensing technical information into "punchy" bullet points and using visual hierarchy to improve information retention.

Module 2: Building a Student Digital Portfolio

Objective: To establish a foundational professional presence across key industry platforms.
Execution: Created and optimized profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle.
Technical Setup: Developed a specialized GitHub Profile README containing personal academic details and a mission statement regarding future learning goals.
Professional Networking: Updated the LinkedIn Education section with current degree details and expected graduation from VIT.

Module 3: Exploring Coding & Collaboration Platforms

Objective: To build familiarity with platforms used for technical practice and cloud-based teamwork.
Part A (Coding): Completed a beginner-level challenge on HackerRank to demonstrate basic programming proficiency and logic.
Part B (Collaboration): Designed a 5-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms.
Data Management: Linked the quiz to a Google Sheet to practice real-time data collection and response analysis.

Module 4: Professional Communication & Etiquette

Objective: To master the art of professional digital correspondence and responsible social media use.
Email Practice: Drafted two high-stakes professional emails—one requesting an assignment extension from a professor and another expressing interest in a summer internship.
Social Media Ethics: Created a "Do’s and Don’ts" checklist to guide college students in maintaining a positive and professional digital footprint.
Reflection: Analyzed the real-world consequences of poor digital communication and how to avoid such pitfalls.

Module 5: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention
Objective: To recognize, describe, and provide prevention strategies for modern online threats.
Case Study: Developed a 200–250 word deep dive into a specific cybercrime, such as Phishing or UPI/Online Payment Fraud, detailing the step-by-step mechanics of the attack.
Prevention Strategy: Compiled a "Stay Safe Online" checklist featuring 8 actionable tips, including specific advice for financial security in India.
Resources: Integrated official reporting channels, including the National Cyber Crime Portal and the 1930 Helpline
